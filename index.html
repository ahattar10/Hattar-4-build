Trip Overview

A desert hiking weekend can be memorable when the schedule stays simple. The best approach is to choose a few well-matched trails, prepare for changing temperatures, and leave enough time to enjoy the scenery instead of rushing through it.

This sample plan is designed for hikers who want a balanced weekend with moderate routes, clear preparation, and time for rest.

Mesa Point Finish

Packing List

Packing the right essentials makes the trip more comfortable and helps prevent common mistakes in hot, dry conditions.

  • Refillable water bottles or a hydration pack
  • Hat, sunglasses, and sunscreen
  • Comfortable hiking shoes with good traction
  • Snacks such as fruit, trail mix, and protein bars
  • Downloaded trail maps in case service is limited

You can also review REI’s beginner hiking guide for additional gear suggestions.

Trail Etiquette

Good trail habits protect natural spaces and make the experience better for other hikers.

  1. Stay on marked paths to avoid damaging desert vegetation.
  2. Yield politely on narrow trail sections.
  3. Pack out all trash, including wrappers and bottles.
  4. Check weather conditions before you leave.
